Matrices

In mathematics, a matrix is a rectangular array of numbers, symbols, or expressions, arranged in rows and columns.

This example matrix A\mathbf{A}A has 3 rows and 2 columns.

A=[510−201−1] \mathbf{A}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}{5} & {10} \\ {-2} & {0} \\ {1} & {-1}\end{array}\right] A=⎣⎡​5−21​100−1​⎦⎤​

Products

A⊙B\boldsymbol{A} \odot \boldsymbol{B}A⊙B : Element-wise(Hadamard) product

[a1a2]⊙[b1b2]=[a1⋅b1a2⋅b2] \left[\begin{array}{l}{a_{1}} \\ {a_{2}}\end{array}\right] \odot\left[\begin{array}{l}{b_{1}} \\ {b_{2}}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}{a_{1} \cdot b_{1}} \\ {a_{2} \cdot b_{2}}\end{array}\right] [a1​a2​​]⊙[b1​b2​​]=[a1​⋅b1​a2​⋅b2​​]

An easy example of Hadamard product:

[510−201−1]⊙[2−1−1501]=[5×210×−1−2×−10×51×0−1×1]=[10−10200−1] \left[\begin{array}{rr}{5} & {10} \\ {-2} & {0} \\ {1} & {-1}\end{array}\right] \odot\left[\begin{array}{rr}{2} & {-1} \\ {-1} & {5} \\ {0} & {1}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{rr}{5 \times 2} & {10 \times-1} \\ {-2 \times-1} & {0 \times 5} \\ {1 \times 0} & {-1 \times 1}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{rr}{10} & {-10} \\ {2} & {0} \\ {0} & {-1}\end{array}\right] ⎣⎡​5−21​100−1​⎦⎤​⊙⎣⎡​2−10​−151​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​5×2−2×−11×0​10×−10×5−1×1​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​1020​−100−1​⎦⎤​
